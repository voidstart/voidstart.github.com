Towards understandng of the routes

Let me layout the gist of routing:

User browser side: Request: HTTP_METHOD + URL HTTP_METHOD: One of GET, POST, DELETE, PUT URL: Something like /products/17

Server side: Needs to decide: Controller, Action, and Params

Example: /products/17 “products” :–> Controller “17” :–> :id (of Params) Now what’s the Action ? (Method to call) Answer: Depends on HTTP_METHOD , which is not part of URL, but part of

the underlying communication from browser. ex. GET -> Show (Quite obvious)

Helpers: In Rails code, both URL and HTTP_METHOD for the generated link can be constructed in some kind of wrapper method like product_path(1). This is part of Rails evolution history.

What’s troubling me is it’s fair enough to understand the explict way of just write out the URL, but sometimes confusing to figure out what exactly some helper would generate.
