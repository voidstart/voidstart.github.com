Whitelist vs blacklist

It’s clever as a security design to disable everything first, then only when needed, add whitelistings for things that do need direct access without authentication.

I first thought the authentication should be put in the models, because no matter how the top structure may be complex to control, the gatekeeper should be at the last defence for the database.

Turns out I was wrong. In Rails, the super class of all controller could be used as the central location of access before all other smaller logic units.

I am still not sure an admin page to “welcome” is really needed as the books says. I am just looking to guard existing functionality with user authentication.
